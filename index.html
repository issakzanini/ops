📋 1 — Introduction, Definitions & Regulatory Framework
Key Definitions
  • MOPSC — Maximum Operational Passenger Seating Configuration
  • MCTOM — Maximum Certified Take-Off Mass
  • Commercial Air Transport — Transporting passengers/cargo/mail for remuneration or other valuable consideration
  • Flight Time — Brakes OFF → Brakes ON
  • FDP — Report time (pre-flight) to end of post-flight duties
  • Critical Phases of Flight — T/O, climb-out, final approach, landing; any time Commander decides
  • OEI — One Engine Inoperative
  • AEO — All Engines Operative
  • Isolated Aerodrome — Has no destination alternate
  • Equivalent Position — Suitable fix 3–5 nm from threshold
Operations Manual Structure
  • Part A — General / Basic (Company-wide)
  • Part B — Type Specific
  • Part C — Aerodromes and Routes
  • Part D — Training
EU Ops Regulation Structure
  • I — Definitions
  • II — ARO — Authority Requirements
  • III — ORO — Operator/Organisation Requirements
  • IV — CAT — Commercial Air Transport
  • V — SPA — Specific Approvals (ETOPS, LVO etc.)
Ops Manual must be followed except in emergencies. PIC is responsible.
Prepared in English; crew must receive Parts A & B for personal study.
Alternate Types
  • Take-off Alternate — Cannot return to departure aerodrome
  • Destination Alternate — Destination is unusable
  • En-route Alternate (ERA) — For in-flight emergencies
  • ETOPS Alternate — For ETOPS emergencies
AOC & Management
  • AOC granted by NAA; allows CAT operations
  • An accident does NOT automatically revoke the AOC
  • Required post holders: Flight Ops, Maintenance, Crew Training, Ground Ops
  • >21 employees = Complex Company
  • Safety Management System (SMS): Recommended MCTOM >20,000kg; Mandatory MCTOM >27,000kg
  • FDM Program required if MCTOM >27,000kg
Leasing
  • Dry Lease — No crew; operated under AOC of lessee. Dry lease-in ≤ 7 months in any 12 consecutive month period (non-EU)
  • Wet Lease — Crew provided; operated under AOC of lessor
MEL vs MMEL
  • MMEL — Made by manufacturer; always less restrictive than MEL; approved by Manufacturer's State Authority
  • MEL — Written by operator; approved by operator's State Authority; found in Ops Manual Part B; only relevant stationary prior to departure
  • MEL must reflect MMEL changes within 90 days
  • Operations outside MEL: authority approval required

🔧 2 — Aircraft Equipment Requirements
Navigation Lights
  • Wing lights: RED (left) / GREEN (right) — 110° arc each
  • Rear (white) light — 140° arc
  • Required at night only; always steady (not flashing)
  • Anti-collision lights (strobes): required in addition
FDR / CVR Requirements
  • Required: all aircraft >5,700kg; all ME turbines with MOPSC >9
  • CVR — Records last 2 hours (30 min for <5700kg / pre-1998)
  • FDR — Records last 25 hours (10 hours if >5700kg)
  • Both start before aircraft moves under own power
  • FDR retained for 60 days after accident/incident
  • Datalink has same requirements as CVR
ELT
  • Transmits on 121.5 MHz and 406 MHz simultaneously
  • Battery lasts 48 hours
  • MOPSC <19 — 1 ELT of any type (post-2008: 1 automatic)
  • MOPSC >19 — 1 automatic ELT or 2 of any type (post-2008: 2 ELTs, 1 auto)

Emergency Medical Kit
  • Required when MOPSC >30 AND >60 mins from aerodrome with qualified medical assistance
  • Contains syringes, defibrillators, drugs
  • Kept secure in flight crew compartment
Crash Axe / Crowbar
  • Required: MCTOM >5700kg OR MOPSC >9 → 1 on flight deck
  • MOPSC >200 → additional 1 in rearmost galley
  • Crash axe can be substituted by a crowbar
Megaphones
  • MOPSC 61–99 = 1
  • MOPSC ≥100 = 2
  • No requirement if aircraft is empty
Spare Fuses
  • Greater of 10% or 3 for each rating
Seats
  • Adult = 12+ years; Child = 2–12; Infant = <2 (no own seat needed)
  • Must be aligned within 15° of longitudinal axis
  • Child restraint device: <24 months
Emergency Lighting
  • Required: MOPSC >19
  • Must last minimum 10 minutes
  • Always ARMED in normal flight
  • General illumination + floor-level lighting + illuminated exit lights required if MOPSC >9
Emergency Slides / Evacuation
  • Required when sill height >1.83m (6ft)
  • Pre-2000: tested with landing gear extended
  • Post-2000: tested with landing gear collapsed
  • Self-contained inflator; manual handle as backup
  • Slide will NOT inflate if opened from outside
  • Evacuation must be possible in 90 seconds through 50% of exits
IFR & Night — Altimetry (2 Systems Required)
  • 2 sensitive pressure altimeters
  • 2 independent static pressure systems (alternate static permitted for props <5700kg)
  • Additional standby attitude indicator if MCTOM >5700kg or MOPSC >9 — must last 30 mins
Radio & Navigation Equipment
  • All comms equipment must be able to transmit on 121.5 MHz
  • VFR with visual references: min 1 radio + 1 transponder
  • IFR: 2 independent radios (each with own antenna) + headset/mic per pilot
  • IFR/VFR no visual refs: 1 VOR + 1 ADF + 1 DME (ADF omitted if not required)
  • VFR without landmarks: 1 VOR + 1 DME
  • ETOPS >180 min: reliable comms (voice or data-link) required
  • 1 equipment failure must not affect any other equipment

First Aid (Therapeutic) Oxygen
  • Required when operating above 25,000 ft
  • Covers remainder of flight where cabin altitude is between 8,000 ft and 15,000 ft
  • Sufficient for 2% of passengers carried, minimum for 1 pax
  • Minimum 2 dispensing units
  • Average flow rate: 3 litres/min STPD (starts at 4, decreases to 2 min)
  • Total dispensing outlets must exceed seats by 10%
Oxygen Mask Requirements
  • 110% of seating capacity: quick-donning masks above 25,000 ft
  • Above FL410: one pilot must continuously wear oxygen mask
  • Oxygen diluter-demand system: pure O₂ supply above FL320
  • PBE (Protective Breathing Equipment): provides O₂ for >15 minutes
  • Utility: checked before taxi, demonstrated before take-off
  • Cabin altitude horn sounds at >10,000 ft
Physiology Key Numbers
  • Maximum cabin altitude for 100% O₂ efficiency: 8,000 ft
  • Pilot physically affected (lack of O₂): from 6,000 ft
  • Crew efficiency not impaired: up to 8,000 ft
  • TUC at FL400: approx. 12 seconds
  • Oxygen drop-out masks: auto-deploy at 15,000 ft
🦺 4 — Survival Equipment, Fire & Contaminated Runways
Life Jackets
  • Required for landplane when >50 NM from shore OR T/O & landing gives likelihood of ditching
  • Sufficient for all on board
  • Must have a locator light
  • Inflated on exiting the aircraft (not inside)
Life Rafts
  • Required when >120 min at OEI cruise speed or 400 nm (lesser) from suitable land
  • Other aircraft: >30 min / 100 nm (lesser)
  • Must assume largest raft is unserviceable → +1 spare of largest rated capacity
Survival Equipment — Remote Areas
  • Exception: within 90 minutes at cruising speed of suitable landing area
  • Otherwise must carry:
    • Signalling equipment
    • At least 1 ELT
    • Additional survival equipment suitable for route

Runway Surface Definitions (EASA)
  • Dry — Neither wet nor contaminated; includes 'effectively dry' sections
  • Damp — Not dry but moisture doesn't give shiny appearance
  • Wet — Appears reflective (shiny) but no significant standing water
  • Contaminated — ≥25% of runway surface covered by ≥3mm of water (or equivalent) in: slush, loose snow, compacted snow, or ice

SNOWTAM
  • Valid for 24 hours
  • Section D — Cleared runway length (4-figure group)
  • Section F — Deposits over total runway length (6 = slush)
  • Section H — Estimated surface friction on each third [5-4-3-2-1]
  • Section T — Plain language remarks (including uncleared parts)
  • 2 figures = measured by device; 1 figure = estimated by pilot
  • Shiny appearance does NOT mean contaminated — requires ≥3mm + ≥25% coverage
  • Slush has the lowest friction coefficient
👨‍✈️ 5 — Crew Requirements, Training & Recency
Flight Crew Composition
  • Minimum 1 PIC at all times
  • Minimum 2 flight crew for IFR/night on turboprops with MOPSC >9 or turbojets
  • Exception: approved single-pilot operations
  • Only 1 inexperienced flight crew member at any time
  • ≥1 suitably qualified pilot at controls during flight
  • Controlled rest permitted if Commander decides (not a qualifying rest)
Cabin Crew Requirements
  • Required when MOPSC >19
  • Minimum age: 18 years
  • 1 cabin crew per 50 seats (or fraction thereof) on same deck
  • Multiple types/variants: max 3 types (4 with authority approval)
Senior Cabin Crew Requirements
  • Minimum 12 months experience (some sources: 1 year)
  • Must complete appropriate training course and skills test
  • Nominated by operator; responsible to Commander
  • May suspend non-safety tasks; may tell Commander to turn on seatbelt sign

Recency Requirements
Commander
  • 3 T/Os and 3 landings in last 90 days on same type/class
  • 1 night landing in last 90 days
  • Can be extended to 120 days with a TRI
  • >120 days → training flight or sim required
Co-pilot
  • 3 T/Os and 3 landings in last 90 days on same class/type (or sim)
Single Pilot IFR
  • 5 IFR flights including 3 instrument approaches in last 90 days in that type/class
ZFTT — Zero Flight Time Training
  • Training conducted solely in the simulator
  • After ZFTT conversion, pilot must:
    • Commence supervised line flying within 21 days of skills test
    • 6 T/Os and landings in sim no later than 21 days after skills test
    • First 4 T/Os and landings on line under supervision
    • Sim and first T/Os with TRI in pilot's seat
OPC Content (must include)
  • Rejected Take-Off
  • Engine failure between V₁ and V₂
  • 3D approach with OEI
  • 2D approach to minima
  • Missed approach (OEI if multi-engine)
  • Landing with OEI or PFL (single-engine)
  • LVO training: at least 4 approaches; includes taxi, T/O, approach, flare, landing, rollout, missed approach
Crew Seat Belt & Harness Rules
  • T/O & Landing: Belts + Harness
  • Other phases: Belts whilst at station
  • Pilots must be strapped in for T/O, landing, and whenever Commander decides
  • Safety belt always fastened while seated
Recurrent Training — Emergency & Safety Equipment
  • All exits, firefighting, pyrotechnics and slides: every 3 years
  • Life vests, extinguishers and PBE: every year
  • CRM major topics: every 3 years
  • Can be revalidated up to 3 months early
⏱ 6 — Flight Duty & Rest Limitations
Key Definitions
  • Duty — Anything done for the operator (except post-flight activities)
  • FDP — Report time to end of post-flight duties
  • Night Duty — 02:00–04:59 in time zone crew are acclimatised to
  • WOCL — Window of Circadian Low: 02:00–05:59 within 3 time zones of base
  • Standby — Counts as duty if subsequently called in; Airport Standby always counts
  • Break — Free of duties but does NOT count as rest period
  • Positioning — Counts as flight duty
  • Home Base — Where crew member usually starts/ends duty

📍 7 — Pre-flight Planning & Alternates
Take-off Alternate
  • Required when impossible to return to departure aerodrome (weather or performance)
  • 2-engine aircraft — within 1 hour OEI cruise speed
  • 3/4-engine aircraft — within 2 hours OEI cruise speed
  • Wx: within planning minima ± 1 hr ETA
  • If no alternate available: conditions must be better than required for landing with available instrument approach (AEO)
Isolated Aerodrome — When Applicable
  • Piston: diversion fuel exceeds lesser of (45 min + 15% cruise flight time) or 2 hours
  • Jet: diversion fuel exceeds 2 hours normal cruise + final reserve
  • Additional fuel = 2 hours normal cruise consumption
Destination Alternate
  • Minimum 1 alternate IFR unless:
    • Flight time <6 hours AND 2+ separate runways with ceiling >2,000 ft AAL or circling height +500 ft AND vis >5 km ±1 hr ETA
    • OR aerodrome is isolated
  • 2 destination alternates required when:
    • Wx below planning minima ±1 hr ETA
    • No met information available at destination
  • Wx: within planning minima ± 1 hr ETA
ETOPS Alternate
  • Wx: within planning minima ± 1 hr ETA
  • At expected time of use must have: ATS facility + at least 1 instrument approach procedure

In-flight Minima — Continuation of Approach
Approach can ALWAYS be commenced regardless of conditions.
  • DA/H < 1,000 ft AAL: Cannot continue below 1,000 ft AAL if RVR/VIS is below minima. If RVR drops after 1,000 ft AAL → can continue to DA/H or MDA/H. If visual parameters not met at DA/H → discontinue.
  • DA/H > 1,000 ft AAL: Cannot continue into final approach segment (OM). Same rule at DA/H — discontinue if visual parameters not met.
RVR Reports & Landing Factors
  • RVR reports issued when RVR falls below 1,500 m
  • TDZ RVR is the controlling value
  • Midpoint minimum: 125 m
  • Stop end minimum: 75 m

🌫 9 — Low Visibility Operations (LVO)
Definitions
  • LVP — Low Visibility Procedures: anything less than CAT I ILS (not inclusive)
  • LVO — Low Visibility Operations
  • LVTO — Low Visibility Take-Off: RVR <400 m
  • CDFA — Continuous Descent Final Approach
  • SAp — Stabilised Approach
LVTO Requirements
  • LVTO: RVR <400 m
  • Cannot go below 150 m RVR (Cat A, B, C) or 200 m (Cat D) without authority approval
  • If no RVR reported: PIC may visually determine RVR
  • ME must be able to stop or continue safely to 1,500 ft after engine failure
Restricted Conditions (NADP)
  • Crosswind (inc. gusts) >15 kts
  • Tailwind (inc. gusts) >5 kts
  • Contaminated runway
  • Adverse weather (windshear etc.)
  • T/O — VIS <1 NM; Landing — ceiling <500 ft AGL
LVO Approval Requirements
  • Operator establishes minima; not lower than State minima
  • For operator validation of DH ≥50 ft: at least 30 approaches/landings
  • For DH <50 ft: at least 100 approaches/landings
  • Must be monitored; reports retained for 12 months
  • Training and checking syllabus approved by Authority
  • All flight crew members must be qualified and current
⛽ 10 — Fuel Policy
Fuel Components
  • Taxi fuel
  • Trip fuel
  • Contingency fuel
  • Alternate fuel (not ETOPS)
  • Final Reserve fuel
  • Extra fuel (discretion of commander)
Must land with Alternate + Final Reserve fuel or it is a FUEL EMERGENCY
Crew responsible for in-flight re-planning of fuel
Final Reserve Fuel
  • Turbine (jets): 30 minutes at holding speed at 1,500 ft above destination
  • Reciprocating (pistons): 45 minutes
Isolated Aerodrome Additional Fuel
  • 2 hours normal cruise consumption (including final reserve)
Fuel Jettison
  • Must jettison sufficient fuel within 15 minutes
  • Should be above FL060 if possible
  • Preferably over water, away from CBs, in a straight line
  • ATC clearance required
  • Jettison when unable to meet 2.7% climb gradient OEI or 3.2% AEO
Refuelling with Passengers
  • Permitted if operator authorised
  • Requires:
    • Qualified person on board with 2-way comms
    • Crew, staff and passengers warned
    • FSB signs OFF; No Smoking ON
    • Internal lights ON; minimum cabin crew on board
    • Emergency slides/exits clear
  • Stop if you smell fuel!
  • Prohibited for wide-cut fuels / AVGAS

🌐 12 — North Atlantic (NAT), MNPS & RVSM
MNPS Airspace
  • Lateral: 27°N to 90°N
  • Vertical: FL285–FL420 (RVSM levels available FL290–FL410)
  • Requires MNPS + RVSM approval
  • ATS Flight Plan Item 10: X = MNPS approved (next to S); W = RVSM approved
  • Class A airspace above FL55 / 2,000 ft AGL
  • Significant points every 10° longitude
  • Above 70°N: position reports every 20° longitude
MNPS Requirements
  • 2 LRNS (Long Range Navigation Systems) for unrestricted operations: IRS / INS / GNSS
  • 1 LRNS only: Blue Spruce/Tango routes (state approval required)
  • All nav equipment accessible from both seats
  • Group altimetry system: ±80 ft
  • Non-group altimetry system: ±200 ft
Mandatory IFR in NAT
  • Above FL060 or 2,000 ft AGL (whichever greater)
OTS — Organised Track System
FlowCross 30°W
DayE→W (Westbound)1130–1900 UTC
NightW→E (Eastbound)0100–0800 UTC
  • Day OTS published: 2200 UTC by Shanwick
  • Night OTS published: 1400 UTC by Gander
  • Aircraft on same track separated by 1,000 ft
  • Active based on timing at 30°W
  • PRM deadline Day: 1900 UTC; Night: 1000 UTC
Oceanic Clearance
  • Request 40 minutes before entry point ETA
  • 3 elements: Route (lateral) + Mach number (longitudinal) + Flight Level (vertical)
  • Report ETA changes of 3 mins or more to ATC
  • Tolerance boundary window: 3 mins
  • Flight plans filed 3 hours before EOBT
NAT Squawk
  • Keep last assigned squawk for 30 min after NAT entry
  • Then squawk 2000 with Mode A/C
  • SELCAL checked at/before MNPSA entry
  • Air-to-air VHF: 123.45 MHz
Mach Number Separation
  • Standard: 10 mins (same Mach number same track)
  • Reduce by 1 min per 0.01 Mach faster (minimum: 5 mins)
  • Turbojet: 15 min same track & same Mach; 5 min absolute min
  • Turboprop: 20 min; 30 min in western NAT area
  • Lateral min: 60 nm standard; 30 nm absolute minimum
SLOP — Strategic Lateral Offset
  • Centreline, 1 nm right, or 2 nm right
  • Never to the left or beyond 2 nm
LRNS Failure Procedures
  • Before boundary: Avoid entry; use Blue Spruce/Tango routes
  • Once within OCA (1 LRNS): Prepare proposal, advise ATC, continue
  • Remaining LRNS failure: Notify ATC immediately; attempt visual sightings; consider climbing/descending 500 ft; broadcast on 121.5/123.45
Unable to Maintain Level
  • Attempt re-clearance first
  • If not possible: offset by 15 nm and change FL:
    • Below FL410: ±500 ft
    • Above FL410: ±1,000 ft
    • At FL410: +1,000 ft or –500 ft
RVSM Requirements & Tolerances
  • 1,000 ft separation between FL290 and FL410
  • 2 altitude measurement systems + 1 altitude alerting + 1 automatic control + 1 SSR transponder

  • In-flight: primary altimeters agree within ±200 ft (max 1 hr intervals)
  • Error tolerance of assigned altitude: ±300 ft
  • Level-off: VS not exceeding 1,500 fpm with 1,500 ft to go
  • Do not overshoot/undershoot by more than 150 ft

  • Turn South → Climb 300 ft

❄️ 13 — Special Operational Procedures (Icing, NADP, Wake Turbulence)
Icing & Ground De-/Anti-icing
  • Commander ensures critical surfaces free of ice/snow/slush/frost prior to T/O
  • Clean Aircraft Concept: ALL contamination removed (fuselage OK)
  • Icing most likely: 0°C to –10°C; rare below –18°C
  • Can reduce lift by 30% and increase drag by 40%
  • Forms on leading edges first
De/Anti-ice Fluid Types
  • Type I — De-ice (Hot / pre-existing ice)
  • Type II — Anti-ice (Cold / preventative)
  • Type IV — Anti-ice (expensive)
Holdover time starts at beginning of LAST step. Must repeat if expired. Jet/prop wash washes off fluid — avoid.
Precipitation Danger (Least → Most)
  1. Frost
  2. Freezing Fog
  3. Snow
  4. Freezing Drizzle
  5. Freezing Rain
  6. Rain-soaked Cold Wing
NADP — Noise Abatement Departure
  • Active from 800 ft (power reduction) to 3,000 ft
  • NADP 1 (Close-In): Climb at V₂ +10–20 kts; flaps retracted at 3,000 ft. Max 15° AOB.
  • NADP 2 (Distant): Flaps up >800 ft. No simultaneous power reduction.
  • No turns at same time as power reduction
  • Min height for power reduction: 240 m (800 ft)
  • At least 500 ft above obstacles
NADP NOT Required When
  • Runway contaminated
  • No ILS/VASI guidance
  • VIS <1 NM
  • Crosswind >15 kts / Tailwind >5 kts
  • Windshear / adverse weather
  • Ceiling <500 ft AGL
  • Aircraft failure
Wake Turbulence
  • Created when aircraft generates lift (nose wheel lifts off)
  • HEAVY: >136,000 kg; MEDIUM: 7,001–135,999 kg; LIGHT: ≤7,000 kg
  • Vortices separate upward around tip and go outward from runway
  • Left = Clockwise; Right = Anti-clockwise
  • Travels horizontally at ½ wingspan height
  • Worst: heavy, clean, slow aircraft in light crosswinds
  • Avoid by flying above and upwind of preceding aircraft
  • Wake dissipation time: 3 minutes

Windshear & Microbursts
  • Windshear: rapid change in wind speed or direction
  • Vertical windshear → turbulence; horizontal → headwind/tailwind change
  • Microburst: Downdraughts in 4 km area for 1–5 mins; high-pressure divergent centre; wind shifts from headwind to equal/opposite tailwind
Windshear Go-Around Procedure
  • Aggressively apply full throttle
  • Pitch up to stick shaker
  • No configuration changes (Speedbrake OK)
Headwind ↑ = Energy ↑ | Tailwind ↑ = Energy ↓
Bird Strikes
  • Highest risk: closest to ground (<2,500 ft / <1,500 ft)
  • Commander must submit written report after landing if bird struck
  • If seen near airport → report immediately to ATS
  • Most effective deterrents: bird distress calls + shell crackers
  • Migration info: AIP ENR 5.6
  • Attracted to: garbage sites, short grass, wet areas, coastal areas, ploughed fields
  • Deterrent: long grass (>20 cm) on airfield

🚨 14 — Emergency Procedures & Security
Decompression
  • First sign of rapid decompression: BANG! followed by mist and cabin altitude increase
  • Slow decompression: caused by leaks
  • First action: don oxygen mask immediately
  • Then emergency descent to 8,000 ft (performance not affected) or MSA
  • O₂ degrades performance above 6,000 ft; pure O₂ required above FL320
Smoke Procedure
  • Immediately apply oxygen mask (100%)
  • Cabin crew attempt to determine source
  • If source cannot be determined → divert
Fire Management
  • Always follow the checklist; always check with PBE (>15 min O₂)
  • Fire after V₁ → Continue take-off
  • Carburettor fire → Mixture OFF, open throttle
  • Tailpipe fire → Dry cranking
  • Toilets → All available extinguishers simultaneously
  • Cargo → Isolate ventilation system
  • Engine → Halon; Cockpit → Halon/CO₂; Wheel-well → Dry powder
Overheated Brakes
  • Approach from front or rear
  • Do NOT set parking brake
  • Use water fog, sand or dry powder
  • Do NOT use water
Ditching
  • Immediately initiate evacuation
  • Life jackets inflated on exiting (not inside)
  • Ditch parallel and on top of waves (except for strong winds)
  • Gear up, flaps down, nose high
Hijacking Response
  • Flight deck door locks only from inside
  • Fly at IFR level: 1,000 ft sep → ±500 ft; 2,000 ft sep → ±1,000 ft
  • Attempt to broadcast warning on VHF / 121.5 MHz
  • State of Registry, State of Operator & ICAO must be notified
Bomb on Board
  • Descend to cabin altitude in landing configuration
  • Will not have to remove suspect packages
Emergency / Precautionary Landings
  • Precautionary — Land at earliest opportunity (bad weather, passenger incapacitation, onset of night, etc.)
  • Emergency — Land immediately (uncontrolled fire, dual engine failure, etc.)
  • Safe Forced Landing: inevitable landing with no injuries expected
  • On runway: stop there and turn off systems
  • Over trees: aim for low trees, flaps down, gear up
Security Definitions
  • Airside — Movement area of airport and buildings with controlled access
  • Screening — Application of means to identify prohibited articles
  • Security Control — Means to prevent introduction of prohibited articles
  • Security Restricted Area — Airside risk-priority area with additional measures
  • Unidentified Baggage — Baggage not picked up or identified by a passenger

⛽ FUEL PLANNING — CAT.OP.MPA.180 / 181 / 182 / 185 (EU 965/2012 as amended by Reg. 2021/1296)
The Three Fuel/Energy Scheme Types — CAT.OP.MPA.180
① BASIC FUEL SCHEME
Standard regulatory fuel quantities. Based on large-scale industry safety data. Applies to all CAT operators by default. Priority: Safe → Effective → Efficient.
Used unless operator applies for a variation or individual scheme.
② BASIC + VARIATIONS
Basic scheme with approved statistical deviations (e.g. statistical taxi fuel, statistical contingency). Each variation must be approved by Competent Authority.
E.g. Statistical contingency fuel method, reduced planning minima.
③ INDIVIDUAL FUEL SCHEME
Operator-specific scheme based on minimum 2 years of own data. Must demonstrate equivalent or higher level of safety vs. basic scheme. Requires authority approval.
Uses SPIs (Safety Performance Indicators) to monitor safety levels continuously.
All fuel schemes must comprise: (1) Fuel planning & in-flight re-planning policy; (2) Aerodrome selection policy; (3) In-flight fuel management policy. Any change to the scheme requires prior CA approval.
Pre-flight Fuel Calculation — Block Fuel = TTCAFED
CodeComponentRegulatory Definition (CAT.OP.MPA.181 — Basic Scheme, Perf Class A)
T Taxi Fuel Fuel expected to be used prior to take-off only (not arrival taxi). Must account for APU consumption, local conditions (LVP, CDM, NOTAMs, weather), and anticipated ground delays. Statistical taxi fuel permissible under basic + variations scheme.
T Trip Fuel From take-off to landing at destination. Must include: (1) climb to initial cruise level; (2) step climbs if applicable; (3) cruise; (4) descent to IAF; (5) approach and landing. For STAR with point merge, include fuel for the direct STAR path. Planned using aircraft-specific fuel consumption data (from FMS/monitoring system) preferred over manufacturer data.
C Contingency Fuel Higher of:
• 5% of trip fuel (or 5% of remaining trip fuel if in-flight re-planning), OR
• 5 minutes holding at 1,500 ft above destination in ISA, at holding speed, at estimated arrival mass.
Reducible to 3% under basic + variations scheme if a Fuel ERA aerodrome is nominated: must lie within a circle of radius = 20% of total route distance, centred at 25% of total distance from destination (minimum 20% + 50 NM from destination).
A Alternate Fuel Fuel for: (1) missed approach from DA/H or MDA/H at destination (full missed approach procedure); (2) climb to cruise level; (3) cruise to alternate; (4) descent; (5) approach and landing at alternate. If 2 alternates required: use the one requiring more fuel. Does NOT include a missed approach at the alternate itself.
F Final Reserve Fuel (FRF) Holding at 1,500 ft (450 m) above alternate (or destination if no alternate) in ISA, at holding speed, at estimated arrival mass:
• Turbine engines: 30 minutes
• Reciprocating engines: 45 minutes
FRF is inviolable. If FRF would be consumed → declare MAYDAY FUEL. If any change to clearance risks consuming FRF → declare MINIMUM FUEL.
E Additional Fuel Required when minimum fuel (T+T+C+A+F) is insufficient to reach a Fuel ERA aerodrome following engine failure or loss of pressurisation at the most critical point on route. Must allow: driftdown to ERA, hold 15 minutes at 1,500 ft above ERA, approach and land.
E Extra Fuel For anticipated delays or specific operational constraints that can be predicted at planning stage. Included at PIC's discretion for comfort margin (e.g. forecast holds, busy terminal, unreliable routing).
D Discretionary Fuel Commander's sole discretion. Not a regulatory requirement. Added when PIC judges additional margin is prudent (e.g. uncertainty in weather, ATC, operational context).
No Alternate Required — Conditions
CAT.OP.MPA.182(e) — All conditions must be met simultaneously
✔ Flight time ≤ 6 hrs (in-flight re-planning: remaining ≤ 4 hrs)
✔ ≥ 2 separate runways usable at destination
✔ Met forecast ETA ±1 hr: ceiling ≥ 2,000 ft (600 m) or circling height +500 ft (150 m), whichever higher
✔ Ground visibility ≥ 5 km during ±1 hr ETA window
When no alternate required — alternate fuel replaced by:
Minimum 15 minutes holding at 1,500 ft above destination at holding speed, ISA (enables a safe landing).
2 Alternates required when:
Met forecast at destination is below planning minima → operator must name a 2nd alternate. Load alternate fuel for the one requiring more fuel.
Isolated Aerodrome — Extra fuel rule:
Jets: 2 hrs at normal cruise fuel consumption (including FRF)
Pistons: Lesser of (45 min + 15% cruise time) or 2 hrs
Isolated aerodrome = no adequate alternate within normal fuel range. PNR (point of no return) must be calculated and not exceeded without current met assessment.

In-Flight Fuel Management — CAT.OP.MPA.185
Fuel checks: At regular intervals (basic scheme: at least every 60 minutes). At each check:
  ① Compare actual vs. planned consumption
  ② Verify sufficient fuel remains to complete flight
  ③ Estimate fuel on landing at destination
Target at destination: Usable fuel on landing must be ≥ Alternate fuel + FRF (or ≥ FRF if no alternate required).
If check shows fuel < Alternate + FRF: Request delay info from ATC; assess whether to proceed or divert to perform safe landing with ≥ FRF.
MINIMUM FUEL declaration: When PIC has committed to land at a specific aerodrome AND any change to clearance may result in landing with < FRF. NOT an emergency — but signals emergency is possible if further delay occurs.

MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY FUEL: When estimated fuel on landing will be less than FRF. This IS an emergency.
Data to be recorded: Off-block fuel, take-off fuel (auto if possible), MINIMUM FUEL declarations, MAYDAY FUEL declarations, fuel after touchdown, on-block fuel.
RCF — Revised Contingency Fuel (Decision Point Procedure)
Allows contingency fuel reduction to 3% by using a Fuel ERA aerodrome. At the Decision Point, calculate two scenarios and carry the higher:
RCF-1 — Continue to Destination 1:
Taxi + Trip (DP→Dest 1) + Contingency 5% (dep→Dest 1) + Alternate fuel (if req.) + FRF + Additional + Extra + Disc.
RCF-2 — Divert to Destination 2 (ERA) via DP:
Taxi + Trip (dep→Dest 2 via DP) + Contingency ≥3% + Alternate (Dest 2, if req.) + FRF + Additional + Extra + Disc.
Load = MAX(RCF-1, RCF-2)
At the Decision Point in flight: Remaining fuel must be ≥ Trip (DP→Dest) + Cont 5% + Alternate + Additional + FRF.
PDP — Point of No Return (Isolated Aerodrome)
Last point from which the aircraft can divert to a Fuel ERA aerodrome. Beyond the PNR, the aircraft is committed to the destination. Flight must not proceed past PNR unless current met/traffic assessment confirms a safe landing is achievable.
At PNR in flight, remaining fuel must be ≥:
Trip (PNR→Dest) + Contingency + Additional (≥FRF for jets / ≥45 min pistons) + Extra + Disc.
Landing Mass & Fuel Calculations
LM at Destination = ZFM + FRF + Alternate + Contingency
LM at Alternate = ZFM + FRF + Contingency (at altn)
ZFM = Zero Fuel Mass (aircraft OEW + payload; no fuel)
LM at destination = what remains after burning trip fuel and taxi from destination.
Must be ≤ Maximum Landing Mass (MLM).
If LM > MLM → must offload payload or reduce extra/discretionary fuel. Tankering may not be possible.
Fuel Tankering
FPR = Price at Departure ÷ Price at Arrival
FPR < 1.0 → fuel is cheaper at departure → consider tankering extra fuel.
FPR ≥ 1.0 → no economic benefit from tankering.
Fuel penalty: Extra fuel carried increases TOW → burns more fuel en-route. Net saving = Gross saving − Penalty cost (typically 3–5% of tanked fuel weight per sector).
Always check: MTOM structural limits, MLM at arrival, and that FRF/alternate fuel is never compromised to accommodate tanker fuel.

🧭 NAVIGATION PLANNING — Points, Levels & Distance Calculations
PET — Point of Equal Time (Equal Time Point)
The point where time to fly on to destination = time to fly home to departure. Used to determine diversion options.
X = (D × H) / (O + H)
X = distance from departure to PET
D = total distance departure → destination
H = groundspeed homeward (return leg, into wind if headwind on outbound)
O = groundspeed onward (forward to destination)
Wind effect: PET always moves into wind — headwind on outbound → PET moves closer to destination; tailwind → PET moves towards departure.
2-engine PET (asymmetric): Use driftdown speed + single-engine GS for the failed-engine leg. Useful for ETOPS planning.
Multi-leg PET: Calculate cumulative time from each end for all legs; PET is where cumulative times are equal.
PSR — Point of Safe Return
Last point from which the aircraft can return to departure with at least the Final Reserve Fuel on landing.
X = (E × O × H) / (O + H)
X = distance from departure to PSR
E = usable endurance (time) available for outbound + return combined
O = groundspeed outbound
H = groundspeed homebound
Endurance E: (Total usable fuel − Final Reserve Fuel) ÷ Fuel flow
Wind effect: PSR always moves towards departure with a headwind (headwind shrinks usable range outbound). Tailwind on outbound → PSR moves further from departure.
PSR is always ≤ PET in distance from departure.
NAM / NGM Conversions
NAM / TAS = NGM / GS
NGM = NAM ± (WC × t / 60)
NAM = Nautical Air Miles — distance through the air mass
NGM = Nautical Ground Miles — actual distance over ground
WC = Wind Component (+ tailwind, − headwind)
t = time in minutes
Range tables are in NAM (air miles). Convert to NGM using GS for actual ground distance flown. Use TAS for NAM, GS for NGM.
1-in-60 Rule:
Track Error (°) = Off-track × 60 ÷ Distance Flown
Closing Angle = Off-track × 60 ÷ Distance to Go
Hdg correction (direct): Track Error + Closing Angle. Valid for angles <15°.
